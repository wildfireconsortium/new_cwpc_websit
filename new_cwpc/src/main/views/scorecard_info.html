Stakeholders

In addition to local community members, example stakeholders include:
  • Local, regional, and national governments
  • Fire, water, vegetation, early detection, and other experts
  • Insurance companies
  • Reinsurance companies/ Insurance linked security companies
  • Utility companies
  • Social enterprises
  • Neighborhood and home-owner associations
  • Developers/real estate/ landowners
  • Community wildfire organizations
  • Local community emergency preparedness associations etc.

All communities are expected to have gaps in catastrophic wildfire community risk reduction. The objective is to anticipate how vulnerabilities to wildfire generate opportunities for catastrophe community risk reduction and a chance to learn from others who have already embraced some of these strategies. We can amplify network effects with accelerated learning from many regions creatively reducing their risks and sharing their results in a community of practice.
The ARISE-US/CrowdDoing CWPC Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard has been released as an open source tool.

Pilot with Us!

Communities wishing to pilot our scorecard are invited to do so in multiple ways:
  • Self-pilot
  • Collaborate with other networks
  • Expert review with support for your collaboration
  • Full collaboration with our consortium of social innovators and experts
