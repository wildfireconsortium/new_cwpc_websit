Press Release - Launching the Scorecard

Groundbreaking New Wildfire Resilience Scorecard Empowers Communities to Assess Wildfire Risk, Boost Preparedness.
Global Rollout Kicks Off With Pilot Program to Test Methodology. Participants Wanted

For Immediate Release
February 19, 2024 - (San Francisco, CA) Communities worldwide now have a new tool to evaluate wildfire risk and strengthen resilience with the introduction of Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard by ARISE-US and CrowdDoing Catastrophic Wildfire Prevention Consortium (CWPC).

The Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard enables assessment of wildfire risk across property, community and landscape scales. Developed collaboratively by wildfire experts, ARISE-US and CWPC, the open-source scorecard provides a robust set of questions to develop a baseline assessment, identify risks, and contribute to creating targeted plans, and driving unified action.

View and download the Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard published by the United Nations Office for Disaster Risk Reductions (UNDRR) PreventionWeb: Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard and Scorecard prevent wildfire.
The Wildfire Resilience Scorecard incorporates cutting-edge resilience principles across land use planning, infrastructure hardening, public education, governance, nature-based solutions, ecosystem management, and post-disaster recovery capacity. This comprehensive approach prepares communities to develop targeted plans to substantially reduce wildfire risks through integrated planning, mitigation, and governance.

Global rollout starts with a pilot program enabling communities worldwide to test and refine the scorecard. In addition to communities, we invite diverse stakeholders - governments, companies, associations - to adopt the Wildfire Resilience Scorecard, collaborate to improve it through feedback and pilots, and build collaborative resilience strategies against increasingly devastating wildfires.

“Community collaboration is critical for preventing catastrophic wildfires. Help us make communities more resilient. Pilot with us. Send feedback, collaborate, share data, learnings and best practices to accelerate our progress.” said Claudia Brenner, Co-CEO CrowdDoing CWPC.

Download, collaborate, send feedback and learn more at http://scorecard.preventwildfire.world/

"By building systems change strategies with global collaborators, we empower communities to understand risks through the scorecard. Together we can reduce catastrophic wildfire risks and build prevention pathways for a hotter, drier, fire-prone world," said Bobby Fishkin, Co-CEO of Catastrophic Wildfire Prevention at CrowdDoing & Co-leader of CrowdDoing.

“Many communities either don’t have the information they need to understand and improve their wildfire risk, or they have too much, in too many places and too complex to understand. The Scorecard is the first piece of a strategy to create a free “one stop shop” for the integrated information and expertise that communities will need. The Scorecard allows a community to see how it is placed currently with respect to wildfire risk. Other pieces will help it select strategies and actions to move forward and improve their risk and resilience,” said Peter Williams, Chair of ARISE-US.

This groundbreaking scorecard is a gift to reduce disaster risk worldwide. Join the movement for resilience. Contact us at contact@preventwildfire.world

ARISE, the Private Sector Alliance for Disaster Resilient Societies, is a network of private sector entities led by the UN Office for Disaster Risk Reduction (UNDRR). In 2015, UNDRR started ARISE to support the private sector to become a key partner in reducing disaster risk. It has grown to over 400 members and over 29 networks across the world. ARISE-US is the US Network of ARISE.

Catastrophic Wildfire Prevention Consortium (CWPC) at CrowdDoing is a cross-sector, international consortium of wildfire experts, institutions, entrepreneurs and volunteers with a goal of preventing catastrophic wildfires through a portfolio of tailored, integrated innovations for communities.
